# 0003. Lean into the Sequoyah heritage as brand depth

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-05-20

## Context

SEQTEK is short for Sequoyah Technologies — named for the Cherokee polymath who designed a writing system that gave his nation near-universal literacy within a generation. Internally there has been ongoing pressure to drop the Sequoyah connection on the grounds that the name doesn't read as "tech" in a five-second skim. The founder (Hank) has consistently held that the story is real, true, and worth telling. ROADMAP `BR-1` has been open since the project began.

`BRAND_STRATEGY_RESEARCH.md` (2026-05-14) synthesized Hinge's _High Growth Study 2024_, the _Visible Expert_ research, Edelman trust data, ten origin-story case studies, and an Oklahoma cultural-appropriation review. The clearest finding: story-based differentiation is the second-ranked priority of high-growth professional-services firms, and "mark as surface, meaning as depth" is the durable pattern across coined B2B technology marks (Atlassian, Asana, Slack, Stripe). The Sequoyah story is unusually well-matched to the firm's value proposition — a system that scales human capability is what a technology consultancy aspires to build. The full research case lives in `BRAND_STRATEGY_RESEARCH.md` §1, §3, §4, §7.

## Options considered

- **Drop Sequoyah entirely.** Treat SEQTEK as a coined mark with no heritage. Lower friction in a five-second skim; throws away the differentiator Hinge identifies as load-bearing for high-growth firms. The rebrand-leader's instinct standing alone.
- **Mention but don't lead.** One footnote on /about; no homepage signal, no cross-content connection. Hedges the question — reads as either ignorance or extraction in the Native-acknowledgement frame. The path of least courage.
- **Lean in as layered brand depth.** SEQTEK is the surface mark on every page; one homepage sentence carries the depth signal; /about carries the full Sequoyah narrative + a cultural acknowledgement; the story is connected to what the firm actually does and threaded through Touchstone Workshops as the "systems that make people more capable" thesis.

## Decision

**We lean in.** SEQTEK stays the primary mark — masthead, footer, social, OG titles, paid media. One homepage sentence carries the depth signal and links to `/about/our-story`. `/about/our-story` carries the 200-400 word Sequoyah origin section plus the cultural-acknowledgement line. The thread connecting Sequoyah and SEQTEK's work — _systems that scale what people can do_ — is also the thesis behind Touchstone Workshops (the three-workshop journey: Five Dysfunctions → Re-Alignment → Case Study). Touchstone exists to align people on systems they can absorb; Sequoyah designed a system that an entire nation could absorb. The brand depth and the methodology say the same thing.

This is a leadership call, not a stakeholder compromise. The reasoning: a layered approach preserves the rebrand-leader's correct instinct (a five-second visitor needs a five-second name) while keeping the differentiator the research is unambiguous on (story-based brands grow faster). It also closes the Native-acknowledgement question explicitly rather than by silence.

## Touchstone connection (the operational thread)

The Sequoyah story is connected to the firm's actual delivery model, not used as decoration. Three places this surfaces in content:

1. **Homepage trust block** — one sentence carries the depth signal. Suggested draft (iterates with the content lead): _"SEQTEK is short for Sequoyah Technologies — named for the Cherokee polymath who built a writing system that gave his nation universal literacy in a generation. We build systems that scale what people can do."_ Link "Sequoyah Technologies" to `/about/our-story`.
2. **`/about/our-story`** — the 200-400 word origin narrative closes by connecting Sequoyah's syllabary to Touchstone Workshops: both are _systems people can absorb and use to do more than they could before_. This is the bridge from origin story to current offering.
3. **`/workshops` landing** — opens with a single line grounding the methodology in the firm's founding thesis: _"We build the same kind of thing our namesake built — systems that scale what people can do."_

The phrasing iterates with the content lead; the structural commitment is the three placements and the one connective thesis.

## Cultural acknowledgement

`/about/our-story` carries an explicit one-sentence acknowledgement. Suggested draft (per `BRAND_STRATEGY_RESEARCH.md` §4): _"We took our name from Sequoyah, a Cherokee polymath born in what is now Oklahoma; we're a non-tribal firm and we honor his legacy by building systems that make people more capable."_ Silence on this question reads as either ignorance or extraction in the Native-acknowledgement frame; one explicit line, paired with a real connection to the work, reads as respect.

## Cherokee Nation outreach

Deferred to pre-launch per ROADMAP `BR-6`. Not a precondition for shipping the brand depth — Sequoyah is a public historical figure honored across Oklahoma without controversy (Sequoyah County, Sequoyah High School, Sequoyah Schools, Sequoyah State Park). A courtesy notification before launch is a goodwill gesture, not a permission gate.

## Consequences

- **Gain:** The strongest available differentiator in the regional consultancy field. None of the comparable firms in `BRAND_STRATEGY_RESEARCH.md` §6 ground their brand in a named founder or origin story. The competitive set is open.
- **Gain:** Touchstone Workshops gain a brand-coherent narrative spine. The same "scale what people can do" idea runs from the firm name → the syllabary metaphor → the workshop journey. That coherence is hard to manufacture and easy to recognize.
- **Gain:** The cultural-acknowledgement language closes the appropriation question explicitly. An explicit one-sentence acknowledgement paired with a real connection to the work reads as respect, not extraction.
- **Gain:** A founder origin story gives journalists, podcasters, and conference organizers a hook. The _Visible Expert_ research is direct that this kind of hook drives the largest single category of professional-services brand equity.
- **Cost:** The About narrative and the homepage acknowledgement need ongoing stewardship. Sloppy execution would be worse than no execution. `CONTENT-REQUIREMENTS.md` §1 holds the copy spec; the content lead owns enforcement.
- **Cost:** A small residual risk that a hostile audience reframes the brand depth as appropriation. The cultural-acknowledgement sentence plus the non-tribal-firm framing in `BRAND_STRATEGY_RESEARCH.md` §4 is the mitigation; we are not insulated from misreadings, only positioned to defend against them in good faith.
- **Cost:** The lean-in choice raises the bar on every adjacent content artifact (case studies, Touchstone copy, `/about/our-story`, leadership bios). Aspirational text and stock-photo decoration become more obviously thin against a real founding story. This is a feature, not a bug, but it means content lift increases.

## Revisit when

- Leadership decides to reposition the firm away from Oklahoma roots or away from the people-and-systems thesis.
- A direct critique of the Sequoyah framing emerges from Cherokee Nation, the Cherokee community, or a credible regional voice — at which point the cultural-acknowledgement copy and the connection framing get reviewed, not removed by default.
- Touchstone Workshops are sunset or rebranded — the "systems that scale what people can do" thread depends on Touchstone being current.
